Murder

When Batavia was wrecked of the Western Australian coast, survivors were forced to make camp on the islands of the Houtman Abrolhos on Morning Reef. During the three months before help arrived, only 116 people out of 300 survived. Visit the gallery and learn about the devastating details of the events following the shipwreck, involving the mutineers behind the horrific schemes. Costing lives of innocent people, including mothers and their children.
