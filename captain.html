Captain's Log

Uncover and learn of Batavia’s horrific history. Take a closer look at the unique pieces stored in our world class collection and learn more about the fates of the Batavia and her 322 passengers and crew.

Batavia Model

Once the proud flagship of the Vereenigde Oostindische Compagnie, the Batavia was a mighty three-masted vessel measuring 56.6 metres in length and boasting a formidable armament of 28 guns. Experience the Batavia in its original glory through the model meticulously created over a 10-year period and generously donated by Geoff Kimpton. Geoff was a former member of the WA Museum who played a major role in the ship’s recovery and the reconstruction of its hull.

Captain's Cabin

Batavia’s skipper, Ariaen Jacobsz slept and lived in the great cabin, which can be described as the nerve centre of the ship. The skipper and his colleagues met here to chart their ill-fated crossing of the Indian Ocean, as their commander, Francisco Pelsaert, lay ill, bed-ridden in his own cabin.

People of Batavia

One of the first Europeans in Australia. A victim of the Batavia mutiny In 1963, shortly after the Batavia was discovered, an expedition to Beacon Island found two skeletons buried in shallow graves in the corral rubbles of the small island. The remains that are displayed are of a male aged between 35 to 39 years old.
